# Testing: "represent me accurately" (no gender push)

Testing whether dropping the masc/fem comparative-push clauses entirely — keeping
only the QUALITY CORE identity/skin/hair rules — lets the model render the
person's actual proportions from the photo, anime-stylized, without pushing
toward either binary gender.

**Not a locked variant yet.** This is a research test, not a third product option —
see the open question in PROJECT_STATUS.md before treating this as decided.

**How to test it (quick, ~15 min):**
1. Run this exact prompt against 3 different reference selfies: one masc-presenting,
   one fem-presenting, one androgynous/ambiguous-presenting if you have one available.
2. For each, run it 2-3 times (no seed control, so check for consistency too).
3. Look specifically for: does the output hold the photo's actual jaw/brow/build,
   or does it quietly drift toward a stereotyped binary anime look anyway despite
   no explicit push? That drift — if it happens — would be model bias, not a
   prompt-wording gap, same category as the skin-tone/head-tilt issues already
   documented.
4. Compare side by side against the existing masc/fem locked outputs for the same
   selfie (sticker #1, crying-laughing) to see how different the result actually is.

Using sticker #1 (crying-laughing) as the test pose since it's already locked/approved,
so this is an apples-to-apples comparison — same pose, only the identity-treatment
clause changes.

Transform the person in this photo into anime style, keeping their facial structure and overall identity clearly recognizable as the same person. STRICT RULE on facial features: preserve their actual nose, lip, and eye proportions from the photo, stylized at the same degree of anime simplification used for any face — do not narrow, thin, or "correct" features toward a generic anime default, and do not exaggerate them either. STRICT RULE on skin tone: identify the person's true base skin tone directly from the darkest, most shadow-neutral area of their actual face in the photo (jawline, under the cheekbone) — not an average of the whole face, and never a bright highlight/glare area, those are NOT the true skin color. Reproduce that exact tone faithfully, whether it is light, medium, tan, brown, or deep dark brown — do not shift it lighter, warmer, or more "generic" than what the photo shows. The model's known failure mode is defaulting any skin tone toward a lighter medium-brown/tan midtone regardless of the source photo; actively resist that pull and match the real photo's tone instead. When cel-shading, keep all shadow and highlight variations within that same base color family via hue shifts (warm reds/oranges or cool blues), never lightening the base tone itself to make it "cleaner" or more visible. The nose must be rendered completely matte and flat-colored, with no shine, highlight, glint, or specular dot on the tip or bridge — flat cel-shading only, no nose highlight of any kind. This is a small sticker so the nose must read as clean, flat color with zero extra marks. The mouth and lips must also be rendered completely matte and flat-colored — no shine, gloss, highlight dot, or specular mark on the lips, teeth, or inside the mouth. STRICT RULE on makeup: identify any visible makeup in the reference photo — lipstick or lip color, eyeliner, eyeshadow, blush, etc. — and preserve it faithfully in the rendering, using colors and placement that match the photo, stylized as flat, matte cel-shading like the rest of the face. Do not remove makeup that's present in the photo, and do not add makeup that isn't there. Flat cel-shading only on the entire face: nose, lips, and cheeks all stay shine-free. The visible top row of teeth must be rendered bright white or off-white, with only light gray or pale blue used for subtle shading between individual teeth for depth — never black, dark gray, or a discolored/dirty tone. Teeth must read as clearly white in the final image. STRICT RULE on hair: first check whether the person in the photo is wearing a hat or head covering that conceals their hair. If so, render the hat or head covering faithfully in the anime style instead — do not guess, invent, or infer a hairstyle for hair that isn't actually visible in the photo. If no hat is worn, check whether the person is bald or significantly balding (a shaved head, a receding hairline, or a bald crown/pattern). If so, preserve that faithfully — render them as bald or balding in the same pattern shown in the photo, do not invent a full head of hair they don't have, this is not a defect to "fix." If the person has visible hair, identify the general category of their natural hair texture from the photo (straight, wavy, curly, or coily) and use it as the foundation — do not literally replicate the exact cut or length from the photo. STRICT RULE on hair color: identify the person's actual hair color directly from the photo (black, dark brown, brown, blonde, red, gray, dyed color, etc.) and reproduce that same color family faithfully — do not substitute a different, more "flattering," or more common anime hair color. STRICT RULE on hair length and style: preserve the reference photo's actual hair length and general styling — do not lengthen, shorten, or restyle it toward a more typically masculine or feminine cut. Design a fully realized, attractive hairstyle true to that texture and length, built as a shaped, layered mass with real volume, styled as if deliberately done. Render the hair (or bald scalp, if applicable) in completely flat, matte cel-shading — no shine, no gloss, no specular highlight strokes, no white rim-light or glow around the silhouette. STRICT RULE on outline: the only outline in the image is the single bold black sticker border traced tightly around the head/hair silhouette — there must be no additional white or light-colored rim-light, halo, glow, or aura effect surrounding the head, and no soft gradient/blur ring around the outside of the hair. The area immediately outside the black outline is plain transparent background, nothing else. STRICT RULE on gender presentation, critical: do not push the person's features toward either a more masculine or more feminine anime look. Preserve the reference photo's actual jawline width and angle, brow ridge prominence, chin shape, and cheek fullness as closely as the anime simplification allows — no widening or narrowing the jaw, no softening or sharpening the brow, no adding or removing implied gender markers beyond what standard anime facial simplification does to every face equally. If the person has facial hair in the reference photo, preserve it in the same style and coverage. This is a faithful anime stylization of the actual person, not a gendered transformation. STRICT RULE on accessories: identify any accessories visible in the reference photo. Include and faithfully render, in correct general shape and color: glasses, hats or head coverings, and any piercings/jewelry at or above the face (nose rings/studs, eyebrow rings/studs) — these sit on the face or head and are part of the person's identifiable look. Omit entirely: earrings, and any jewelry or accessory below the neck (necklaces, chains, watches, bracelets, rings on the hands) — these do not get rendered. Completely REPLACE only their facial expression with the one described here — ignore the expression in the photo. The expression is the crying-laughing emoji brought to life: head in a perfectly flat, straight-on frontal angle exactly like an emoji icon — no tilt, no chin-up or chin-down angle, no 3/4 perspective, camera looking directly at the face dead-center, eyes squeezed tightly shut drawn as upward-curved arcs, huge wide-open laughing mouth showing the top row of teeth, eyebrows raised high. Exaggerate the tears comedically: two big cartoonish fountains of tears bursting sideways from the outer corners of both eyes like little geysers, oversized teardrops flying outward and away from the face with comic motion. The mood is loud hysterical joy. Extreme close-up crop: head only, cropped right at the neck — no neck, shoulders, chest, or clothing visible at all, just the face and hair filling the frame. Simple bold clean linework, soft flat cel-shaded coloring, minimal detail. No text, no logos, no watermarks. Output as a clean sticker: transparent background, bold outline, square, high resolution. FINAL CHECK before rendering: the only water/tear shapes in the entire image are the two fountains flying outward from the outer corners of the eyes — the face, nose, mouth, teeth, cheeks, chin, and hair must otherwise be completely clean, flat, and matte, with no extra dots, beads, spots, moles, freckles, shine, glow, discoloration, or marks anywhere that aren't part of the reference photo. Confirm the jawline, brow, and chin shape match the reference photo's actual proportions, not pushed toward a more masculine or feminine anime look.
